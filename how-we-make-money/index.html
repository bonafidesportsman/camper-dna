How the Site is Funded

CamperDNA is free to use and editorially independent. Product recommendations in our guides help fund the site. Here's how that works.

The short version

CamperDNA is free to use. The profiling tool, the guides, the advice — all of it, no charge, no sign-up required.

The site is funded by product recommendations in our kit guides. When you click a clearly labelled link and make a purchase, the retailer pays us a small commission. The price you pay is exactly the same either way — the commission comes out of the retailer's marketing budget, not your pocket.

How the product recommendations work

Our kit guides recommend things we know well — gear we've used, seen on the road, or researched thoroughly. When there's a good place to buy something, we link to it. Those links are labelled clearly, and they help keep the site running.

We only recommend things we'd put in front of a friend asking for advice. We don't write about products because they have an affiliate programme. We don't change a recommendation because one retailer pays a higher commission than another. The recommendation comes first; the link follows.

The tool stays separate

The CamperDNA profiling tool has no commercial logic in it. The spec it generates is based entirely on your answers — not on what any retailer, conversion company, or brand would prefer you to buy. We can't influence that output commercially, and we wouldn't want to.

The guides are where product links appear. The tool is where honest profiling happens. Those two things stay separate.

Which retailers we work with

  • Amazon Associates — kit, gear, accessories and smaller items
  • Specialist outdoor and campervan retailers — where the product is better found elsewhere
  • Insurance and breakdown services — in the buying guides

We'll update this list as the site grows, and any new partnerships will be disclosed clearly.

A note on conversion companies

CamperDNA has no commercial relationship with any conversion company, and no conversion is promoted over another. But we're genuinely supportive of the conversion industry — the whole point of the tool is to help people understand what they want, which makes them better, more confident clients for whoever builds their van.

If you run a conversion company and want to chat about how CamperDNA fits into your customer journey, get in touch.
