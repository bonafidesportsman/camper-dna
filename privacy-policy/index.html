Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 2026

Privacy policies are not exactly the highlight of anyone's day. But if you've taken the time to do our questionnaire, you've shared a bit about yourself with us — your travel style, your budget, how you like to wake up in the morning. That matters, and we think you deserve to know exactly what we do with it.

We collect only what we need, we don't sell your data, and we're not in the business of cluttering your inbox. The longer version is below, written as plainly as we can manage.

1. Who We Are

CamperDNA is an independent website. We help people find the right campervan setup for their lifestyle through a free questionnaire tool and related educational content. For data protection purposes, we are the data controller for personal information collected through this site.

2. What Data We Collect

2.1 Questionnaire Responses

When you complete our campervan profile questionnaire, we collect the responses you provide. These may include information about your travel habits, budget preferences, lifestyle, and campervan priorities. This data is used solely to generate your personalised profile result.

2.2 Email Address

If you choose to receive your profile report by email, we will collect your email address. Providing your email is entirely optional — you can view your results on-screen without submitting it.

2.3 Usage Data

We automatically collect certain technical data when you visit the site, including IP address (anonymised), browser type and version, pages visited and time spent on the site, referring website or search term, and device type and operating system. This data is collected via Google Analytics 4 and is used in aggregated, anonymised form to understand how people use the site and to improve our content.

2.4 Cookies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to operate the site and analyse usage. See Section 7 (Cookies) for full details.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use the data we collect to: generate and display your personalised campervan profile result; send you your profile report by email if requested; analyse site performance and user behaviour in aggregated, anonymised form; improve the questionnaire, content, and overall user experience; and comply with our legal obligations.

4. Legal Basis for Processing (UK GDPR)

Under UK GDPR, we rely on the following legal bases: legitimate interests — for analysing site usage in aggregated form to improve our service, where this does not override your rights; and contract performance — for delivering your questionnaire results and email report when requested.

5. Affiliate Links & Third Parties

CamperDNA earns revenue through affiliate links in our educational content. When you click an affiliate link and make a purchase, we may receive a commission at no additional cost to you. All affiliate relationships are clearly disclosed. We do not share your personal data with affiliate partners or advertisers.

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. We may share data with the following trusted third parties solely to operate the site: Google Analytics (for anonymised website analytics), email service providers (to deliver your profile report, if requested), and hosting and infrastructure providers. All third-party processors are required to handle your data securely and in accordance with applicable law.

7. Cookies

We use essential cookies (required for the site to function — these cannot be disabled) and analytics cookies (used to understand how visitors use the site via Google Analytics — only set with your consent). When you first visit the site, you will be asked for your cookie preferences. See our Cookie Policy for full details.

8. Data Retention

We retain your data only for as long as necessary: questionnaire responses are session-based only unless you provide your email for a report; email address and report data is retained until you request deletion; anonymised analytics data is retained per Google Analytics default retention settings (up to 14 months).

9. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of your personal data; restrict or object to processing; and data portability. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at: campfire@camper-dna.com. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

10. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data. Our site is served over HTTPS and we work only with reputable third-party providers. However, no method of internet transmission is 100% secure.

11. International Transfers

Some third-party service providers (such as Google) may process data outside the UK or EEA. Where this occurs, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, such as Standard Contractual Clauses, in line with UK GDPR requirements.

12. Children's Privacy

CamperDNA is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the 'Last updated' date at the top of this page.

14. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle your data, please get in touch: campfire@camper-dna.com

We'll never do anything with your data that we wouldn't be comfortable telling you about over a brew at the campsite. The campfire is always on.
