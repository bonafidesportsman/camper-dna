How It Works

CamperDNA asks you the right questions, then builds you a personalised campervan spec. Free, independent, no agenda. Here's how it works.

The problem with campervan research

If you've spent any time on campervan forums, Facebook groups or YouTube, you'll know the feeling. You ask a simple question — what size van do I need, fixed bed or rock and roll, T5 or T6 — and you get twenty different answers, each one confident, half of them contradicting each other.

That's not because the community isn't helpful. It's because the right answer genuinely depends on you — how you'll use the van, who's coming with you, where you'll go, what you actually need versus what sounds appealing. A forum can't know that. CamperDNA can.

What CamperDNA does

CamperDNA is a free profiling tool. You answer a set of questions about how you plan to use a campervan — trip length, travelling companions, where you'll stay, what seasons you'll use it — and it builds you a personalised spec: the van type, the layout, the systems, the priorities.

The output isn't a shopping list or a sales pitch. It's a clear picture of what a van built around your actual life looks like. You can use it to guide your own research, hand it to a conversion company as a brief, or simply get some clarity before you spend serious money.

It takes about five minutes.

Step 1 — Choose your starting persona

Before the questions begin, you pick a starting persona. This isn't a commitment — it's a starting point that shapes which questions you're asked and how they're framed.

The five personas are:

Family Adventurer — trips with kids and possibly a dog, school holidays and weekends, campsite-friendly

Weekend Escaper — frequent short trips, couple or solo, year-round use, compact and practical

Off-Grid Explorer — remote destinations, outdoor activities, no hookups, robust power and water systems

European Road Tripper — extended tours, cross-border travel, comfort for long drives, compliance awareness

Curious Beginner — not sure yet, just investigating, needs education before decisions

If you're genuinely not sure, start with Curious Beginner. The flow is designed to explain concepts as it goes, and it ends with a recommendation of which route suits you.

Step 2 — Answer the questions

The questionnaire covers six core areas:

  • Usage frequency — how often you'll realistically use the van shapes everything from battery sizing to storage priorities
  • Trip length — weekend trips and two-week European tours need very different water tanks, power systems and kitchen setups
  • Who travels with you — sleeping layout, seating requirements and space planning all depend on this
  • Where you'll stay — campsite-dependent or off-grid changes your entire electrical specification
  • What seasons — year-round use in the UK has heating and insulation implications that summer-only use doesn't
  • Where you'll travel — UK only or Europe affects insurance, breakdown cover and compliance requirements

Questions are presented one at a time. Each one has a tooltip explaining why it matters and what the implications of each answer are. You're never just clicking options blindly.

Step 3 — Get your profile

At the end, CamperDNA generates your profile. This includes:

  • The van type and specification most suited to your use case
  • Recommended layout (fixed bed, rock and roll, pop-top or high-roof)
  • Power system sizing — battery capacity, solar, split charge
  • Heating recommendation and priority level
  • Water system guidance
  • The key trade-offs specific to your profile — where to spend, where to save

The on-screen summary gives you the headline spec. Your full report — which goes into much more detail on each system, explains the reasoning behind each recommendation and flags the common mistakes for your profile — is delivered by email.

What CamperDNA isn't

It isn't a van dealership. It isn't a conversion company. It isn't an affiliate machine dressed up as a tool.

The recommendations are based on the answers you give, not on what anyone is paying to be recommended. Where we do use affiliate links — clearly labelled in our guides — they help us cover the cost of running the site without charging you anything.
