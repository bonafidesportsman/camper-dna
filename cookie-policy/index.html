Cookie Policy

Last updated: February 2026

Cookies are small text files that websites place on your device. This policy explains which cookies CamperDNA uses, why, and how you can control them.

We keep our cookie use minimal. We don't use cookies to track you across the web or build advertising profiles without your knowledge.

1. The Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These are required for the site to work. They handle things like remembering your cookie preferences so we don't ask you every time you visit. You cannot opt out of essential cookies — without them, parts of the site won't function.

Analytics Cookies (Google Analytics 4)

We use Google Analytics 4 to understand how people use CamperDNA — which pages are popular, how people move through the questionnaire, and where visitors come from. This helps us improve the site over time. All data collected by Google Analytics is anonymised. We don't use it to identify individual visitors. These cookies are only set if you accept analytics cookies when the banner appears.

Advertising Cookies

We do not currently use advertising cookies. CamperDNA may introduce display advertising in future to help sustain the site. If we do, we will update this policy and our consent banner before that happens, and you will be asked for fresh consent at that point.

3. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

When you first visit CamperDNA, a banner will ask for your cookie preferences. You can accept all cookies, accept essential cookies only, or customise your choices. You can change your preferences at any time via the 'Cookie Settings' link in the site footer.

You can also manage or delete cookies directly through your browser settings: Chrome (Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies), Firefox (Settings > Privacy & Security), Safari (Preferences > Privacy), Edge (Settings > Cookies and site permissions). Note that blocking all cookies may affect how CamperDNA functions, particularly the questionnaire tool.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are set by third-party services we use (such as Google Analytics). We don't control how those third parties use the data collected by their cookies. We'd encourage you to review Google's privacy policy for more detail on how GA4 data is handled.

5. Changes to This Policy

We'll update this policy whenever we make meaningful changes to how we use cookies. The 'Last updated' date at the top of this page will change when that happens.
